Signs You Need Help Now

  • No hot water or inconsistent temperature — Could be a failed heating element, thermostat issue, or sediment buildup blocking heat transfer.
  • Water heater leaking from the base — A leaking tank usually means internal corrosion. This requires prompt attention to prevent water damage.
  • Popping, rumbling, or banging noises — Sediment has settled at the bottom of your tank and is being superheated. This is common in Fort Walton Beach due to hard water and should be addressed before it damages the tank lining.
  • Rusty or discolored hot water — Rust-colored water from your hot taps means your anode rod is spent or the tank itself is corroding internally.
  • Hot water runs out faster than it used to — Sediment buildup reduces your tank's effective capacity. A flush may solve it; if the tank is old, replacement may be the better move.
  • Visible corrosion on the unit or connections — Salt air in Fort Walton Beach accelerates external corrosion on tanks, fittings, and gas connections — especially units in garages or outdoor utility closets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

General rule: if your tank water heater is under 8 years old and the repair cost is reasonable, repair makes sense. If it's over 10 years old, leaking from the tank, or requires a major component replacement, a new unit is usually the better investment. We'll give you an honest recommendation.

How long does a water heater last in Fort Walton Beach?

Tank water heaters typically last 8-12 years, but Fort Walton Beach's hard water and salt air can shorten that to 6-8 years without regular maintenance. Tankless units last 15-20 years with proper descaling. Regular flushing and anode rod replacement are the best ways to maximize lifespan.

Is a tankless water heater worth it?

For many Fort Walton Beach homeowners, yes. Tankless units last longer, eliminate standby energy loss, and provide continuous hot water. The upfront cost is higher, but the long-term savings and lifespan often justify it — especially in a coastal environment where tank units corrode faster.

How often should I flush my water heater?

Every six months in the Fort Walton Beach area. Our water is harder than most, and sediment builds up faster. Regular flushing prevents efficiency loss, strange noises, and premature failure.

My water heater is in my garage. Is that a problem in Fort Walton Beach?

Garage-mounted water heaters are common here, but they're more exposed to humidity and salt air. Inspect your unit regularly for external corrosion, especially on gas connections and electrical terminals. A corroded gas connection is a safety hazard.
