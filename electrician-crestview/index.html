Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to keep using aluminum wiring in my older Crestview home?

Aluminum wiring itself isn't immediately dangerous, but it expands and contracts more than copper, which loosens connections over time and creates fire risk. If your home was built in the 1960s-70s and has aluminum wiring, a licensed electrician should inspect your connections. We can install specialized connectors or recommend partial or full rewiring depending on the condition.

Do I need a permit for electrical work in Crestview?

Most electrical work beyond simple fixture swaps requires a permit in Okaloosa County. Panel upgrades, new circuits, rewiring, and generator installations all need permits and inspections. We handle the permitting process as part of the job.

How much does an electrician cost in Crestview?

Costs depend on the scope of work. A straightforward outlet repair costs less than a panel upgrade or whole-house rewire. We provide upfront pricing after evaluating the job — you'll know the cost before any work begins.

Should I get a whole-house surge protector?

In Crestview, yes. Summer thunderstorms produce frequent lightning, and power grid surges are common after outages. A whole-house surge protector installed at your electrical panel protects your HVAC system, appliances, and electronics for a fraction of what it costs to replace them.

What should I do if an outlet sparks or feels warm?

Stop using it immediately. Unplug everything from the outlet and don't touch it with wet hands. A sparking or warm outlet can indicate loose wiring, an overloaded circuit, or a failing outlet — all potential fire hazards. Call for same-day service.
