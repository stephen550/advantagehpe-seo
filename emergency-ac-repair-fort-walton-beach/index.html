This is the most common time for compressor failures — and the worst possible time to be without cooling. If your outdoor unit is humming but no cool air is coming from your vents, the compressor may have failed. **Capacitor burnout.** Capacitors store the electrical charge that starts your compressor and fan motors. Florida heat degrades capacitors faster than moderate climates, and they often fail without warning. A dead capacitor means your AC won't start at all. This is one of the most common — and most quickly fixable — emergency repairs we handle. **Frozen evaporator coil.** Low refrigerant, restricted airflow, or a dirty coil can cause ice to form on your evaporator coil. When the ice builds up enough, your system stops cooling entirely. This is especially common in homes near Okaloosa Island and along the Santa Rosa Sound, where salt air accelerates coil degradation. **Condensate drain backup causing water damage.** Fort Walton Beach humidity means your AC pulls gallons of water out of the air every day. When the condensate drain line clogs — from algae, mold, or debris — that water backs up and can flood your air handler closet, attic, or ceiling. This type of failure causes both an AC shutdown and potential property damage. **Electrical failures after storms.** Summer thunderstorms and power surges are common along the Emerald Coast. A surge can damage your compressor, capacitor, control board, or thermostat. If your AC stopped working after a storm or power outage, call for emergency service rather than repeatedly cycling the system — you could cause further damage. **Blower motor failure.** If your outdoor unit is running but there's no air coming from your vents, the indoor blower motor may have failed. Without the blower, cooled air sits in your system and never reaches your living space. --- ### Why Call Advantage for Emergency AC Repair - **24/7 availability** — We answer emergency calls nights, weekends, and holidays.
