Frequently Asked Questions

How long does generator installation take?

Most installations are completed in one to two days. If your electrical panel needs an upgrade or your fuel line requires significant work, it may take an additional day.

Do I need a permit to install a generator in Fort Walton Beach?

Yes. Okaloosa County requires electrical and gas/plumbing permits for permanent generator installations. We handle all permitting and inspections as part of the job.

Natural gas or propane — which is better?

If your home has natural gas service, natural gas is typically the better choice — unlimited fuel supply and no tank to refill. If natural gas isn't available in your area, propane is the standard alternative. We'll recommend the right fuel source based on your home's existing infrastructure.

How long can a standby generator run continuously?

On natural gas, indefinitely — as long as gas service is maintained. On propane, runtime depends on tank size and generator load. A 500-gallon propane tank can typically power a 22 kW generator for 3-5 days at moderate load.

Will a generator power my central AC?

Yes — that's one of the primary reasons Fort Walton Beach homeowners install standby generators. We size your generator specifically to handle your AC system along with your other critical loads. Keeping your AC running during a summer outage isn't a luxury here — it's a safety issue.
