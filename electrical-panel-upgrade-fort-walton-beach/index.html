Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a panel upgrade take?

Most 200-amp upgrades are completed in one day — typically 6-8 hours. More complex jobs involving service entrance replacement or extensive rewiring may take an additional day. We'll give you a clear timeline before starting.

Do I need a permit for a panel upgrade in Fort Walton Beach?

Yes. Okaloosa County requires an electrical permit for all panel upgrades, and the work must pass inspection. We handle the full permitting process — application, scheduling the inspection, and closing the permit — as part of the job.

Will upgrading my panel fix frequent breaker tripping?

In most cases, yes. If your breakers trip because your panel is overloaded or your breakers are worn out, a new 200-amp panel with properly sized breakers will resolve the issue. If tripping is caused by a wiring problem elsewhere in your home, we'll identify that during our evaluation.

My insurance company said I need to replace my panel in 30 days. Can you meet that deadline?

Yes. We work with Fort Walton Beach homeowners facing insurance deadlines regularly. We prioritize these jobs, handle Okaloosa County permitting, complete the upgrade, and provide the documentation your insurer needs — all within your deadline.

Is my Federal Pacific panel really dangerous?

Yes. Federal Pacific Stab-Lok breakers have been independently tested and found to fail to trip during overloads at rates between 60-80%. This means the breaker that's supposed to protect your home from an electrical fire may not work when it matters. Every major Florida insurer flags these panels, and replacement is strongly recommended regardless of insurance requirements.
