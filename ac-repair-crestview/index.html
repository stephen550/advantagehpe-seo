Signs You Need Help Now

  • AC running but your house isn't cooling — With Crestview summer highs in the low 90s and no coastal breeze, a struggling system falls behind fast.
  • Heat pump not switching modes properly — Heat pumps are the dominant system type in Crestview. If yours isn't transitioning between heating and cooling correctly, the reversing valve or defrost controls may need repair.
  • Short cycling — Your system starts and stops every few minutes. This is hard on the compressor and often points to a refrigerant issue, oversized system, or failing component.
  • Water leaking around your air handler — Clogged condensate lines are common even inland. Crestview's humidity still produces heavy condensation, especially in attic-mounted systems.
  • Strange noises — Grinding, banging, or buzzing from your indoor or outdoor unit means something mechanical is failing.
  • Rising energy bills — A system losing efficiency shows up on your bill before you notice a comfort problem.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you repair heat pumps?

Yes. Heat pumps are the most common system type in Crestview, and we repair all components — compressors, reversing valves, defrost boards, auxiliary heat strips, and more. We service all major brands.

We just PCS'd to Crestview. Should we get our HVAC inspected?

Yes — especially if you bought a resale home. An inspection tells you the system's age, condition, refrigerant type, and whether it's properly sized for the house. Catching issues before your first Crestview summer saves you from an emergency call in July.

How often should I service my AC in Crestview?

At least once a year in the spring. If you have a heat pump, a second visit in the fall before heating season is a smart move. Our Andy's Advantage Plan covers annual maintenance.

Is my AC system the right size for my Crestview home?

Possibly not — especially if your home was built by a production builder. An undersized system won't cool your home on the hottest days, and an oversized system short-cycles and doesn't dehumidify properly. We can evaluate your system's sizing with a load calculation.

My new construction home is only a few years old. Does it really need AC maintenance?

Yes. Builder-grade systems benefit from early maintenance to catch installation issues, verify refrigerant charge, and clean coils that have accumulated pollen and debris since the home was built. Starting maintenance early extends the system's useful life.
