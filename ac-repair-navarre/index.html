Signs You Need Help Now

  • AC running but your home still feels humid — Navarre's average humidity exceeds 70% for most of the year. If your AC isn't pulling moisture out of the air, something is wrong with your system's dehumidification cycle.
  • Warm air from vents — Low refrigerant, a failing compressor, or a frozen coil. In Navarre's heat, this becomes unbearable within hours.
  • System cycling on and off every few minutes — Short cycling wears down your compressor and drives up your power bill. It often points to an oversized system, low refrigerant, or a failing component.
  • Strange sounds from your outdoor unit — Grinding, rattling, or screeching usually means a fan motor issue, loose hardware, or debris in the condenser.
  • Water leaking around your air handler — Clogged condensate drain lines are one of the most common service calls in Navarre. The humidity produces heavy condensation, and the drain line clogs with algae and debris.
  • Your energy bill spiked without explanation — A system working harder than it should will show up on your bill before you notice anything else wrong.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does salt air really affect my AC on Navarre Beach?

Yes. Salt corrodes condenser coils, electrical contacts, and refrigerant lines. Homes on the barrier island need more frequent professional inspections than mainland homes. We check for corrosion on every service call for beach-side properties.

How often should I service my AC in Navarre?

At least once a year — in the spring before cooling season starts. If you're on Navarre Beach or your system is over 10 years old, twice-yearly service is a smart investment. Our Andy's Advantage Plan covers annual maintenance.

Should I repair or replace my AC?

If your system is under 10 years old and the repair cost is reasonable, repair usually makes sense. For systems over 12-15 years — especially those using R-22 refrigerant — replacement is often the better long-term investment. We'll give you an honest recommendation. See our replacement options.

Absolutely. If you've just moved to the area — especially into a resale home — an AC inspection identifies issues before they become summer emergencies. We'll check your system's age, condition, refrigerant type, and overall performance so you know exactly what you're working with.
